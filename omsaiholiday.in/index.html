Special Group Departure Package | Char dham Yatra

Day 1: Arrival in Haridwar 

Upon arrival at Haridwar Railway Station, you will be transferred to your hotel for an overnight stay.


Day 2: Haridwar - Barkot / Sayanachatti (210 kms / 7 hrs) 

After breakfast, proceed to Barkot and check-in at your hotel. The rest of the day is at leisure to acclimatize yourself before starting a trek to Yamunotri from JanakiChatti.


Day 3: Barkot / Sayanachatti - Jankichatti - Yamunotri - Jankichatti - Barkot / Sayanachatti (38kms/2hrs drive & 5kms/3hrs on foot) 

In the morning, leave for Yamunotri where you can cook rice in Garam Pani Kund. Later, take a bath in Jamunabai Kund's hot water and have darshan of pious Yamunaji. Return to the hotel for an overnight stay.


Day 4: Barkot - Uttarakashi /Gangnani (140 Kms / 7 hrs) 

After breakfast, leave for Uttarkashi and visit Kashi Vishwanath Temple before driving to Gangnani. The evening is free for leisure, followed by dinner and an overnight stay in Uttarkashi.


Day 5: Uttarkashi / Gangnani - Gangotri - Harsil - Gangnani / Uttarkashi (120Kms / 5 hrs) round trip 

Early in the morning, start driving to Gangotri for offering prayers and pooja darshan. Later, drive back to Uttarkashi, en route visit Gangnani, followed by an overnight stay at Gangnani or Uttarkashi.


Day 6: Uttarkashi - Guptakashi (250 Kms / 8-9 hrs) 

After breakfast, drive to Sitapur and stay overnight at a hotel.


Day 7: Guptakashi - Gaurikund - Kedarnath - Guptakashi (10 Kms/ 1/2 hrs & 16 Kms/ 6-7 hrs) 

After an early breakfast, leave by vehicle for Gaurikund and join other pilgrims for a 16 km trek to Kedarnath. After darshan and pooja at Kedarnath Temple, return to the hotel for an overnight stay.


Day 8: Kedarnath - Guptakashi 

Return to the hotel and spend the rest of the day at leisure.


Day 9: Sitapur - Pipalkoti 

After breakfast, check out of the hotel and drive to Pipalkoti for an overnight stay.


Day 10: Pipalkoti - Badrinath - Pipalkoti 

After breakfast, start the drive to Badrinath for darshan at Badrinath Temple. Later, return to Pipalkoti for an overnight stay.


Day 11: Pipalkoti - Haridwar 

After breakfast, check out of the hotel and drive to Haridwar for an overnight stay.


Day 12: Haridwar Dropping 

After breakfast, check out of the hotel and depart for your onward journey.

Significance of Yatra

 Char Dham Yatra (4dham yatra) is a pilgrimage tour in Uttarakhand that covers four holy sites, including Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. This Yatra (4 Dham Yatra) is believed to wash away sins and lead one towards Moksha. The Yatra takes place from April to November, with each site having its own significance and unique rituals. Devotees travel by road to each location, experiencing the breathtaking views of the Himalayas along the way. It is considered one of the most significant and spiritually uplifting journeys in India.

CHARDHAM YATRA FROM HARIDWAR

9 NIGHT / 10 DAYS

 

Day 1: Haridwar - Rishikesh - Barkot 

Arrive at Haridwar and proceed to Rishikesh for Yatra Registration. After registration, drive to Barkot (216 km). Check-in at the hotel and rest for the night.


Day 2: Barkot - Yamunotri - Barkot 

Early morning, drive to Janakichatti (approx. 40 km) and then trek for 5 km to reach Yamunotri temple. You can choose to go on foot or by pony/doli (at own expense). Return to Barkot and stay overnight.


Day 3: Barkot - Uttarkashi 

After exploring the place till lunch, drive to Uttarkashi (80 km). Check-in at the hotel and relax for the night.


Day 4: Uttarkashi - Gangotri - Uttarkashi 

Early morning, drive to Gangotri (100 km) and take a holy dip in the Ganges. Perform Pooja and return to Uttarkashi for the night stay.


Day 5: Uttarkashi - Guptkashi/Rampur 

In the morning, drive to Guptkashi/Rampur (230 km). Check-in at the hotel and relax for the night.


Day 6: Guptkashi/Rampur - Kedarnath - Guptkashi/Rampur 

Early morning, drive to Sonprayag (4 km) and then to Gaurikund (5 km) by Govt. Jeep. From there, proceed to Kedarnath (17 km) by pony. After Darshan/Pooja at the temple, return to Guptkashi/Rampur for the night stay. You can also take a helicopter ride to Kedarnath (at own expense).


Day 7: Guptkashi/Rampur - Pipalkoti 

After breakfast, drive to Pipalkoti and relax for the night.


Day 8: Pipalkoti - Badrinath 

After breakfast, drive to Badrinath (80 km) and visit Mana village, the last village before the Indo-Tibetan border. Return to Badrinath for evening Arti and temple Darshan. Stay overnight in Badrinath.


Day 9: Badrinath - Srinagar 

In the morning, perform Pind-Pradanam at Bram Kapal and then drive to Srinagar. Check-in at the hotel and stay overnight.


Day 10: Srinagar - Rishikesh - Haridwar 

Drive to Haridwar via Rishikesh (100 km). Visit and explore Rishikesh, take a holy bath in the Ganges and attend the evening Aarti at Hari-Ki-Pauri. After that, drop at the railway station for further journey.

Significance of Yatra

 Char Dham Yatra (4dham Yatra) is a pilgrimage tour in Uttarakhand that covers four holy sites, including Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. This Yatra is believed to wash away sins and lead one towards Moksha. The Yatra(4 dham Yatra) takes place from April to November, with each site having its own significance and unique rituals. Devotees travel by road to each location, experiencing the breathtaking views of the Himalayas along the way. It is considered one of the most significant and spiritually uplifting journeys in India.

CHARDHAM YATRA FROM DELHI

11 NIGHT / 12 DAYS

 

Day 1: Departure from Delhi to Haridwar

At 9:30 PM, depart from Delhi for Haridwar, which is approximately 205 kilometers away. This will be a night journey.


Day 2: Haridwar to Barkot via Rishikesh 

Upon arrival in Haridwar, freshen up at the Choultry or hotel. Then, proceed to Rishikesh, which is about 24 kilometers away, for yatra registration. After lunch, head to Barkot, which is approximately 216 kilometers away and takes around 7 hours to reach. Overnight stay in Barkot.


Day 3: Barkot to Yamunotri and back 

Depart for Janakichatti, which is about 40 kilometers away, to reduce your trekking distance to 5 kilometers. Proceed to the Yamunotri temple by walking, pony, or doli. The approximate charges for horse rides are between 800-1200 INR, while doli rides cost between 2500-3500 INR. Return to Barkot for an overnight stay.


Day 4: Barkot to Uttarkashi 

You are free to explore until lunchtime, after which you will proceed to Uttarkashi, which is about 80 kilometers away. Check into the hotel upon arrival and stay overnight in Uttarkashi.


Day 5: Uttarkashi to Gangotri and back 

Early in the morning, depart for Gangotri, which is approximately 100 kilometers away and takes around 5 hours to reach. Upon arrival, take a dip in the sacred Ganges, perform pooja, and return to Uttarkashi for an overnight stay.


Day 6: Uttarkashi to Guptkashi/Rampur 

Depart in the morning for Guptkashi/Rampur, which is about 230 kilometers away. Upon arrival, check into the hotel for an overnight stay.


Day 7: Guptkashi/Rampur to Kedarnath and back 

In the early hours, proceed to Sonprayag, which is about 4 kilometers away, and then to Gaurikund, which is approximately 5 kilometers away, by government jeep. Later, proceed to Kedarnath, which is approximately 17 kilometers away, by pony. After darshan/pooja at the temple, return to Rampur for an overnight stay. Pilgrims going by helicopter can proceed to Sirsi/Phata/Guptakashi helipad in the early morning and take a walk to Kedarnath temple (1 km). After darshan/pooja at the temple, return to the helipad and board the helicopter back to Rampur for an overnight stay. 


*You may also take helicopter tickets for Kedarnath at an approximate cost of 9500 INR per person to avoid the trek.


Day 8: Guptkashi/Rampur to Pipalkoti 

After breakfast, enjoy leisure time and proceed to Pipalkoti for an overnight stay.


Day 9: Pipalkoti to Badrinath 

Depart for Badrinath, which is approximately 80 kilometers away. Upon arrival, visit Mana village, which is the last village before the Indo-Tibetan border, and then return to Badrinath to check into the hotel. Visit the temple for evening aarti and darshan. Overnight stay in Badrinath.

 

Day 10: Badrinath to Srinagar

After the morning darshan of Shri Badrinathji and performing Pind-Pradanam at Bram Kapal, we will drive 190 km to Srinagar. Upon arrival, we will check into our hotel and enjoy an overnight stay.


Day 11: Srinagar to Haridwar to Delhi

Today we will depart for Haridwar, which is 125 km away. Along the way, we will visit Rishikesh's Shivananda Ashram, Ram Jhoola, Swarg Ashram, and Geeta Ashram. In the evening, we will take a holy bath in the Ganges at Hari-Ki-Pauri before proceeding to Delhi, which is 205 km away, after dinner. We will travel through the night.


Day 12: Arrival in Delhi

We will arrive in Delhi early in the morning, around 4 am, and be dropped off at the railway station or airport with sweet memories of our journey.

Significance of Yatra

 Char Dham Yatra is a pilgrimage tour in Uttarakhand that covers four holy sites, including Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. This Yatra is believed to wash away sins and lead one towards Moksha. The Yatra takes place from April to November, with each site having its own significance and unique rituals. Devotees travel by road to each location, experiencing the breathtaking views of the Himalayas along the way. It is considered one of the most significant and spiritually uplifting journeys in India.

CHARDHAM YATRA BY HELICOPTER

4 NIGHT / 5 DAYS

 

Yatra starts from the capital of Uttarakhand, the queen of the hills, Dehradun. These four different pilgrim destinations, the Char Dhams, situated in the magical Himalayas, joined by mysticism and spirituality, beckon millions of visitors every year for a voyage into the unknown, for a flight towards the heavens, for a journey into the self…

* Helicopter trip starts from Dehradun.

* Complimentary stay, pickup and Airport transfer for 1 Night in Haridwar/ Dehradun.

* Includes stay & meals for 4 Nights / 5 Days.

* All local transport and sightseeing by Toyota Innova.

* VIP Darshan at all Four temples i.e. Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath and Badrinath.

* Exclusive Maha Abhishek Puja at Badrinath.

* Palki at Yamunotri.


DAY WISE ITINERARY


DAY 1 -> DEHRADUN TO YAMUNOTRI

07:00 AM -> DEPARTURE S’DHARA HELIPAD , DEHRADUN

07:45 AM -> ARRIVAL KHARSALI HELIPAD, YAMUNOTRI

Passengers are requested to reach state government helipad on Sahastradhara Road, Dehradun which is the starting and ending point of this yatra. Vehicles are arranged for pick up.

Our pilots and ground staff will brief the guest about the entire trip and do’s and don’ts. will be arranged at Kharsali and passengers are supposed to leave their hotel early at Dehradun/Haridwar. Upon arrival at Kharsali (The helipad for Yamunotri) passengers are welcomed by Heritage Aviation (HA), ground staff, who will unload their baggage and carry it to your hotel.

Temple is a 6 KMS trek which will be cover by palki. Total journey will take approx. 5 hour 30 min. Generally passengers will return to hotel by lunch time. After rest, We do visit to Shani Temple which is near to your Hotel.


DAY 2 -> YAMUNOTRI TO GANGOTRI

07.45 AM -> Departure from YAMUNOTRI HELIPAD

08.30 AM -> Arrival at HARSIL HELIPAD, GANGOTRI

Harsil is one of the most beautiful and picturesque valleys in Uttrakhand and also known as mini Switzerland. Gangotri temple is approx 22 KMS from Harsil helipad and drive by car is approx. 50 mins.
Stay at Harsil is arranged at Himalayan Nature Resort which is around 2 KMS from helipad. Our ground staff will assist passengers through out there stay at Harsil. We provide all the basic and leisure facilities for the tourists such as a premium accommodation with ensuite washrooms, delicious vegetarian food, Short walks, local sightseeing and an evening bonfire in Gangotri Valley.


DAY 3 -> GANGOTRI TO KEDARNATH

09.00 AM -> Departure from HARSIL HELIPAD, GANGOTRI

10.00 AM -> Arrival at KEDARNATH HELIPAD

Kedarnath is flanked by breathtaking snow-capped peaks, making it the most remote site among all four Char Dham sites.
We will arrive to phata helipad from Gangotri, here after changing the chopper we will fly for KEDARNATH Helipad. Temple is approx. 500 meters away from helipad and will take approx. 15 min to reach.
Kedarnath doesn’t have night halt facility, once the Darshan is completed, We will return to Guptkashi and stay is arranged at Guptkashi / Sitapur.
Passengers can visit ancient Triyuginarayan or Kalimath Mandir in the evening.


DAY 4 -> KEDARNATH TO BADRINATH

0700 AM -> Departure from KADARNATH HELIPAD, BADRINATH

0745 AM -> Arrival at BADRINATH HELIPAD

Badrinath has an average elevation of 3,100 metres. According to legend Shankara discovered a black stone image of Lord Badrinarayan made of Saligram stone in the Alaknanda River.
After arrival, passengers can check in to Hotel Sarovar Portico which is close to temple. In evening , We do Visit of mana village which is near to religious site of Badrinath.
The special Maha Abhishek Pooja is the highlight of this entire yatra. It starts at 0400AM sharp in the morning and last for 2 hours. We have arranged an opportunity for all our guest to sit inside the temple and cherishes the darshan of the Shringar of the deity from close quarters.


DAY 5 -> BADRINATH TO DEHRADUN

11.30 AM -> Departure from BADRINATH HELIPAD

12.30 PM -> Arrival at S’DHARA HELIPAD, DEHRADUN

(Total Cost Per Person is Rs.1,75,000/-) For Min 5 Pax

*GUIDELINES FOR PASSENGERS** There is very limited space for luggage in the chopper. Only 5 kg luggage per passenger will be permitted. Passengers bringing excess baggage would not be allowed to take the same in the chopper.
* Passengers should carry only handbags as suitcases cannot be kept in the helicopter.
* All temples in Chardham are located in high altitude areas. Passengers are Advised to carry regular medical kit.
* At these locations, it becomes cold throughout, so passengers must carry Woollen clothes with them.
* Children above the age of 2 years will be considered full ticket
* There is no provision of concession to children or senior citizens
* All properties lodge with hot water, towels, shower and western toilets.


*TERMS AND CONDITIONS** Available Hotel properties at Yamunotri and Kedarnath are non-star category and the stay is arranged at best amongst them. However at Harsil and Badrinath the accommodation standard is 3.5 Star.
* The booking has to be done in advance with a deposit of 60% of the charter amount.
* Parthtrip Planners reserves the right to postpone the timings of flying subjected to weather conditions.
* Full amount has to be deposited 25 days prior to date of journey.

Benefit of Helicopter for Yatra

 Talking helicopter service provides various benefits for Chardham Yatra, including reduced travel time, increased safety, convenience, and comfort. It eliminates the need for a strenuous trek and offers a bird's eye view of the scenic landscapes. It also saves energy and reduces physical exhaustion, making it an ideal option for elderly and physically challenged individuals. The service is relatively expensive but can be a worthwhile investment for those seeking a hassle-free and comfortable pilgrimage experience.

Significance of Kedarnath Yatra

 Kedarnath Dham is a significant pilgrimage site in the Himalayas dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the four sacred sites of the Char Dham Yatra and is believed to be the place where Lord Shiva absolved Pandavas from their sins. The temple is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty and is accessible by trekking or helicopter. It holds great importance for Hindus and attracts a large number of devotees every year.

Significance of Do Dham Yatra

 The Do Dham Yatra is significant because it allows pilgrims to visit two of the four sacred shrines in the Indian state of Uttarakhand: Kedarnath and Badrinath. Both these shrines are considered highly sacred and are believed to be important pilgrimage destinations for Hindus. The yatra is considered spiritually significant and is believed to offer blessings and divine grace to those who undertake it with devotion and faith. Additionally, the yatra provides a chance to witness the natural beauty of the Himalayas and the scenic landscapes of Uttarakhand.
